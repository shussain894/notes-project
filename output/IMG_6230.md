# When Prayers Are Forbidden

Even in the three when salah is forbidden, a person can do **qada** of salah.  
- **Ahmad:** "Whenever Dhuhr or Asr should pray when he remembers."
- **Hanafi:** Says no.

## Three Times
1. **After Fajr**  
   - **Ahmad:** "Whoever catches one rakah of Fajr or Asr has caught it."
2. **Zawal**
3. **After Asr**

---

## Five Times

1. After Fajr (*salah*) – until sun rises
2. From when sun has risen until sun rises a *spear's length* above the ground
3. Zawal
4. After Asr
5. When sun is about to set until it sets

---

**Abdullah Ibn Abbas:**  
- Some rode, not testified, best of them was Umar.
- **Malik:** Prohibits salah after Fajr & Asr until sun sets.

---

**Uqbah Ibn Amir**
- *Malik*: Forbade us praying at 3 times & burying the dead:
  1. When sun was rising
  2. Zawal
  3. When sun is about to set

---

**Khariji:**  
- You can pray your *nafl* at all 5 times *(1 opinion of Imam Ahmad)*

---

**Imam Malik narrates from Umar**
- He done *tawaf*, didn't pray, went somewhere else & prayed sunnah.
- **Umar** would hit those who prayed at these times.  
  *(Abu Dawud, Abu Darda was hit)*

---

**Khariji:**  
- Safer to not pray at these times.

---
